Judging a Book by its Title...

A data visualization story by Jack Manning


We all know or have heard the phrase “don’t judge a book by it’s cover”, but what books it’s title? As a lover of fantasy and avid reader I stumbled upon a pattern in the books on the shelves of my local library, finding a lot of them fell into the structure of blank of blank: game of thrones, way of kings, color of magic, wheel of time, etc. And the more I thought books popular and beloved series the more this pattern seemed to emerge. I was fascinated, hooked into finding the truth, and to uncover what other patterns might be out there for book titles to follow.

I embarked on this journey with the help of the Goodreads API, a wonderful source of all things nerdy books that provided a list of books and their information on the scale that I had hoped. I was able to collect roughly 15,000 Fantasy and Sci-Fi titles to do analysis on, and after some extensive cleaning and data organization I had a dataset rich with interesting trends and unique quirks. Scroll down below to begin discovering what the world of Fantasy book titles has to offer!

Blank of Blank books

This trope is the largest group of titles I could find, including 2,729 titles, a total of 18.8% of all the Fantasy and Sci-Fi titles that were included in the dataset.

Not only did it have the largest quantity it also has some of the best quality books, according to popularity. The two most famous fantasy fandoms, Game of Thrones and Lord of the Rings, both fall into this trope. And their new TV shows, House of Dragons and Rings of Power respectively, also fall victim to this common title structure.

Other notable inclusions are...

  • Life of Pi
  • Way of Kings
  • Throne of Glass
  • The Chronicles of Narnia
  • Name of the Wind

One Word titles

This is the second largest category of books, coming in with 2,636 book titles, 18.2% of the overall dataset. This trope was far larger than I had expected as there are only so many words that an author can use to entice a reader.

Despite this there are some extremely well-known titles in this category, including...

  • Dune
  • Twilight
  • Divergent
  • Watchmen
  • 1984
