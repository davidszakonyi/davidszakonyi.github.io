ATLS 4519/5519 - Data Storytelling

Fall 2022 Final creative

Students taking Data Storytelling spent the Fall 2022 semester learning books techniques for finding data and telling interesting stories with data. They workshopped story ideas, found data, and collaborated on narrative development. The final project for the course was a fully designed, web-based story that implemented scrollytelling and tackled interesting questions.

The course relied heavily on The Pudding's wonderful resources, up to and including the guidance around how they select stories:

  • The idea is worthy of public discourse. Would people debate the premise of the idea for 20 minutes? What assumptions does it challenge?
  • There's a deeper truth. What does the story reveal (even if it's buried deep in the essay)? Does the reader leave the essay feeling differently?
  • You're showing, not telling. Visuals make your argument more accessible and less complex than a thousand-word essay.
