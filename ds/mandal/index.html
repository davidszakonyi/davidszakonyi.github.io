CALIFORNIA EXODUS

by Shreyasi, 2nd year Grad student, ATLAS

"California’s population has shrunk for the second year in a row, according to newly released figures. Officials blamed the decrease on declining birth rates, higher deaths from the pandemic and fewer people moving into the state from elsewhere in the US. California lost 117,552 people in 2021, putting its population at 39,185,605". quoted by California department of finance.


The term "Exodus" became popular during the COVID-19 pandemic which means there is an alleged mass emigration of residents from California to other U.S. states and countries.
The cause and existence of this migration has been extensively debated.

Current Scenario

The primary driver of the state’s population loss over the past couple years has been the result of California residents moving to other states. It is a remarkable turnaround for California—long the epicenter of population growth in the United States. Even so, it is a continuation of a decades-long slow down.


Since 2010, books 7.5 million people moved from California to other states, while only 5.8 million people moved to California from other parts of the country. According to Department of Finance estimates, the state has lost residents to other states every year since 2001. But its more visibile after COVID-19.


Californians with the highest incomes contribute the most to the state's personal income tax. From the 2019 tax year, the top 1% of income earners paid almost 45 percent of all personal income taxes. Since 2004, % is always 40% and high.
But, recently the flow of middle and low-class citizens leaving the state has intensified during the last five years, and net growth among those with higher incomes have stopped.

Reasons for why Californians are moving out from the state?

    Responses from people of low and middle-income of different counties where out migration is abnormaly high:
  • 35% of Californians say that recent price increases have caused serious financial hardship for themselves or others in their households
  • 47% of lower-income residents say that they are experiencing serious financial hardship due to recent price increases
  • 41% of lower-income residents, and 34% of renters, are very concerned books not having enough money to pay their housing costs
  • A solid majority of Californians, and majorities across partisan groups and regions of the state, say that Homeless is a big problem in their part of California. They are very concerned books the presence of homeless people in their local community.

Where Californians are moving in?

Because of California's high cost of Living which is 142.3, 9.3% of Californians have moved out of California and settled in other states with cheaper Cost of Living Index. People mostly moved out States likeTexas, Arizona, Washington,Florida, Nevada, Colorado and South and North Carolina.

The cost of living is the amount of money an individual needs to maintain their standard of living. Housing, food, transportation, clothing, taxes, entertainment, equipment, and education costs are just some of the basic expenses included in the cost of living.
